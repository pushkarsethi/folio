FAQs

Common
Questions

Absolute return is the total % gain/loss on an investment. CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) normalises this over the years held. A 50% gain over 5 years equals a CAGR of ~8.45% — not 10%. CAGR is always preferred for comparing investments held over different time periods.

For equity in India: STCG (held <12 months) is taxed at 15%. LTCG (held >12 months, gains above ₹1 lakh) is taxed at 10% without indexation. For debt funds/FDs, gains are added to income and taxed at your slab rate.

Divide 72 by the annual interest rate to estimate how many years it takes to double your money. At 12%/yr → doubles in ~6 years. At 6%/yr → ~12 years. This applies to compound interest investments.

Inflation erodes purchasing power. A 12% nominal return with 6% inflation gives a real return of only ~5.66%. In 20 years, ₹1 Crore at 6% inflation is worth only ~₹31 Lakh in today's money. Always plan with inflation in mind.

XIRR (Extended IRR) is the most accurate return measure for investments with multiple cash flows at different times — like a SIP. Unlike CAGR which assumes a single lump sum, XIRR accounts for the timing of each monthly contribution. It's the standard metric mutual funds use.

We use standard financial formulas (A = P(1+r/n)^nt for CI, month-by-month simulation for SIP) following Indian tax laws (2024). These are projections — actual returns depend on markets, fund expenses, and future tax changes. Consult a SEBI-registered advisor for personalised advice.

A Step-Up SIP increases your monthly contribution by a fixed % each year, aligned with salary hikes. Starting at ₹10,000 with a 10% step-up means ₹11,000 in year 2, ₹12,100 in year 3, and so on. This can boost your corpus by 50–80% more than a flat SIP over the same period.
